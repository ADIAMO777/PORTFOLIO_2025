How I Built SwiftCore Solutions' Business Website

Case study · HTML, CSS, JavaScript · Netlify

SwiftCore Solutions operates in two spaces — solar energy services and technology training. The client needed a single website that could communicate both offerings without confusing visitors. My goal was to deliver a professional, fast-loading site that builds trust and drives inquiries.

Understanding the requirements

Before writing code, I mapped the core pages: services, pricing, training programs, and contact. The solar side needed clear product tiers (panels, inverters, battery storage). The training side needed program descriptions and pricing for web development courses. Mobile usability was essential — most Nigerian users browse on phones.

Building the solution

I used a mobile-first approach with semantic HTML for structure and CSS Grid/Flexbox for layout. JavaScript handled interactive elements like navigation and form-ready sections. I kept the stack lightweight (no framework) for fast load times and easy maintenance. The design uses a clear visual hierarchy: hero → services → pricing → contact.

Deployment and outcome

The site was deployed on Netlify with continuous delivery from GitHub. The result is a cohesive business platform that represents both sides of SwiftCore's operations. The client can point potential solar customers and training students to one professional URL.

Key takeaway: For small business sites, clarity beats complexity. Structure the content first, ship fast, and iterate based on client feedback.
